Warning Signs for Emergency Tree Service

Recognizing the warning signs early can save you time, money, and prevent significant damage to your property. Here are the most common indicators that it's time to call a professional:

  • A tree has fallen on your home, car, fence, or other structure
  • Large limbs are hanging precariously and could fall at any moment
  • A tree is leaning severely and appears ready to topple
  • Storm damage has left broken branches dangling over walkways or driveways
  • A tree or branch is resting on or near power lines
  • Root upheaval is causing a tree to tilt dangerously
  • You hear cracking sounds from a tree during or after a storm

How to Prevent Emergency Tree Service Problems

Taking preventive measures can help you avoid costly tree issues down the road. Here are our expert recommendations:

  • Have a certified arborist inspect your trees annually for structural weaknesses before storm season
  • Remove dead branches and deadwood proactively â€” dead limbs are the first to fall in storms
  • Thin dense canopies to allow wind to pass through rather than catching like a sail
  • Address root issues, soil erosion, and poor drainage that weaken tree stability
  • Identify and remove trees that are dead, dying, or structurally compromised before they fail
  • Cable and brace trees with structural defects that you want to preserve rather than remove
  • Know your trees' species â€” some are more prone to storm failure than others
